# Famous Paper Redesign -- Content Strategy

## Thesis

The most famous papers in science have the worst Graphical Abstracts. The higher the citation count, the less effort went into visual communication. This is provable with GLANCE -- and the proof is the content.

The format is simple: take a famous paper, score its GA (or lack of GA) with the full GLANCE pipeline, redesign it parametrically with the VEC methodology, score the redesign, and publish the side-by-side with the delta.

Every redesign is a 200-word diagnosis + a before/after image. Every diagnosis ends with: "Download the template. Score yours."

---

## What It IS

- **Content marketing** that proves GLANCE is actionable, not theoretical
- A demonstration that VEC redesigns produce measurable comprehension improvements
- A lead magnet funnel: free side-by-side content -> template download (29 EUR) -> GA Audit (99 EUR) -> VEC service (850+ EUR/day)
- A dual-voice editorial series: Aurore (clinical/immuno angle) and Nicolas (data/perception angle)

## What It ISN'T

- Not a design service -- we do not redesign GAs for free
- Not a critique of authors -- every diagnosis respects the science and blames the format, never the researcher
- Not an art portfolio -- every redesign is parametric (generated by `compose_paper_ga.py` architecture), not hand-drawn

## 3 Categories

### A: Famous Papers

Papers with 1000+ citations whose GAs are mediocre or absent. The fame makes the content shareable. The audience knows the paper, has never thought about its GA, and is surprised by the score.

### B: Worst Scored

Papers whose GAs score lowest in the GLANCE pipeline. These are not famous -- they are specimens. The content is "GLANCE found this in the wild" and the redesign shows the biggest delta.

### C: Never Had a GA

Landmark papers published before GAs existed (Watson & Crick 1953, Lowry 1951). The content is: "What if this paper had a Graphical Abstract?" Pure demonstration of VEC on canonical data.

---

## Guard-Rails

### Never criticize authors

The diagnosis always says "the format failed the data" or "the encoding compressed the hierarchy." Never "the authors made a poor design choice." The scientist is always respected. The visual encoding is what gets scored.

### Respect copyright

- Category A/B: original GA images are shown under fair use (commentary/criticism, non-commercial educational purpose, small portion of the work, no market substitution)
- Category C: no original GA exists, so no copyright issue
- All original images are attributed with full citation (authors, journal, year, DOI)
- Redesigns are original works generated by the VEC pipeline

### Fair use checklist (per redesign)

1. The original GA is shown for the purpose of critical commentary
2. The original is a small portion of the full paper
3. The use is transformative (scored, analyzed, redesigned)
4. No market substitution (we are not selling the original paper)
