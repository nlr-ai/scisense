Great question about GA design! We've been working on a framework called [GLANCE](https://glance.scisense.fr) that measures whether a Graphical Abstract communicates its key finding in 5 seconds — which is roughly the scroll-speed attention window for most readers.

{context_specific_paragraph}

A few evidence-based principles that might help:
- **30-word maximum** — text beyond this threshold competes with visual encoding (Cleveland & McGill, 1984)
- **Hierarchy clarity** — can a reader identify the main finding without reading any text?
- **Channel efficiency** — position and length encode data more accurately than area or color (Stevens' power law)

If you'd like to test your GA, [GLANCE is free to use](https://glance.scisense.fr) — it gives you a diagnostic archetype and specific recommendations.
