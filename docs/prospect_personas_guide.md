## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

### Primary Target: Affaires Scientifiques et Médicales

SciSense's ideal customers are professionals working in **Medical Affairs**, **Scientific Affairs**, or **Regulatory Affairs** within:

| Company Type | Why They Need SciSense |
|-------------|------------------------|
| **Pharmaceutical laboratories** | Deep study analysis, scientific communication, medical strategy |
| **Biotechnology companies** | Evidence generation, publication support, regulatory dossiers |
| **Vaccine manufacturers** | Clinical data interpretation, safety communication, KOL engagement |
| **Medical device companies** | Scientific positioning, regulatory documentation |
| **CROs with medical affairs services** | Overflow capacity, specialized expertise |
| **Healthcare institutions** | Research support, scientific communication |

### Company Size Sweet Spot
- **Mid-size pharma/biotech** (500-5,000 employees): Often need external support but can't justify large in-house teams
- **Growing specialty pharma** (100-500 employees): Building medical affairs capabilities
- **Large pharma regional affiliates**: Country-level medical affairs with budget autonomy

## Prospect Personas

### Persona 1: The Medical Affairs Head (Decision Maker)

**Titles:**
- VP Medical Affairs
- Head of Medical Affairs
- Global Head of Medical Affairs
- Director, Medical Affairs
- Directeur des Affaires Médicales

**Characteristics:**
- Budget authority for external partnerships
- Oversees MSL teams, medical information, publications
- Reports to CMO or General Manager
- Typically 10-20+ years experience

**Pain Points:**
- Resource constraints during product launches
- Balancing global strategy with local execution
- Evidence generation timelines
- KOL engagement quality

**Where to Find:**
- LinkedIn: Search "Head Medical Affairs" + [company name]
- Company websites: Leadership/About Us pages
- Conference speaker lists (DIA, MAPS, AMCP)
- Press releases announcing appointments

**Qualifying Questions:**
- Do they have products in late-stage development or recent launches?
- Are they expanding into new therapeutic areas or geographies?
- Have they recently announced partnerships or acquisitions?

---

### Persona 2: The Global Medical Product Lead

**Titles:**
- Global Medical Product Lead
- Medical Product Manager
- Global Medical Director, [Therapeutic Area]
- Medical Brand Lead

**Characteristics:**
- Owns medical strategy for specific product(s)
- Cross-functional coordination (R&D, Commercial, Regulatory)
- Evidence generation and publication planning
- Typically 8-15 years experience

**Pain Points:**
- Publication timelines and journal submissions
- Competitive intelligence and differentiation
- Launch readiness and medical education
- Real-world evidence generation

**Where to Find:**
- LinkedIn: Search "Global Medical Product Lead" + [therapeutic area]
- Congress author lists (check abstracts/posters)
- ClinicalTrials.gov (medical monitor contacts)
- Company press releases about product approvals

**Qualifying Questions:**
- Is their product facing competitive pressure?
- Are there recent clinical data readouts to communicate?
- Are they preparing for regulatory submissions or launches?

---

### Persona 3: The MSL / MSL Lead

**Titles:**
- Medical Science Liaison (MSL)
- Senior MSL
- MSL Lead / MSL Manager
- Field Medical Director
- Responsable MSL

**Characteristics:**
- Field-based scientific engagement
- KOL relationship management
- Scientific education and communication
- Typically PhD, PharmD, or MD background

**Pain Points:**
- Keeping up with scientific literature
- Creating compelling scientific content
- Preparing for KOL meetings
- Congress preparation and follow-up

**Where to Find:**
- LinkedIn: Search "MSL" + [therapeutic area] + [country]
- Company career pages (MSL job postings indicate team size)
- Medical affairs conferences (MAPS, MSL Society)
- LinkedIn groups: MSL Society, Medical Affairs Professionals

**Qualifying Questions:**
- Are they in a growing therapeutic area?
- Do they cover multiple products (capacity constraints)?
- Is their company launching new products?

---

### Persona 4: The Scientific/Medical Affairs Project Manager

**Titles:**
- Chef de projet affaires scientifiques et médicales
- Medical Affairs Project Manager
- Scientific Affairs Manager
- Chargé(e) de projets médicaux

**Characteristics:**
- Coordinates medical projects and timelines
- Works with external vendors and agencies
- Publication and congress planning
- Typically 5-10 years experience

**Pain Points:**
- Managing multiple concurrent projects
- Vendor coordination and quality control
- Timeline management
- Resource allocation

**Where to Find:**
- LinkedIn: Search "projet affaires médicales" or "Medical Affairs Project"
- Company LinkedIn pages (employees section)
- Job postings (indicate hiring needs)

**Qualifying Questions:**
- Do they have multiple products in development?
- Are they preparing for major congresses?
- Have they posted job openings (indicating capacity needs)?

---

### Persona 5: The Medical Writer / Publications Manager

**Titles:**
- Medical Writer
- Publications Manager
- Scientific Writer
- Rédacteur médical

**Characteristics:**
- Creates scientific content (manuscripts, abstracts, regulatory docs)
- Manages publication planning
- Works with authors and journals
- Typically PhD or scientific background

**Pain Points:**
- High volume of writing requests
- Timeline pressures for submissions
- Quality and consistency across documents
- Regulatory writing vs. promotional writing balance

**Where to Find:**
- LinkedIn: Search "Medical Writer" + [company] or [therapeutic area]
- ISMPP (International Society for Medical Publication Professionals) membership
- Author lists on published papers
- AMWA (American Medical Writers Association) directory

**Qualifying Questions:**
- Does their company have a large publication portfolio?
- Are they preparing for major regulatory submissions?
- Have they published multiple papers recently (capacity indicator)?

---

### Persona 6: The Regulatory Affairs Specialist

**Titles:**
- Regulatory Affairs Manager
- Chargé d'affaires réglementaires
- Regulatory Affairs Specialist
- Head of Regulatory Affairs

**Characteristics:**
- Manages regulatory submissions and compliance
- Interfaces with health authorities
- Scientific/clinical documentation
- Typically 5-15 years experience

**Pain Points:**
- Submission timelines and quality
- Scientific writing for regulatory dossiers
- Responding to agency questions
- Keeping up with regulatory changes

**Where to Find:**
- LinkedIn: Search "Regulatory Affairs" + [therapeutic area/country]
- RAPS (Regulatory Affairs Professionals Society) membership
- Company regulatory announcements
- Health authority meeting disclosures

**Qualifying Questions:**
- Do they have products in regulatory review?
- Are they expanding into new markets (new submissions)?
- Have they received Complete Response Letters or questions?

## Qualification Criteria

### Scoring Matrix

| Criterion | High (3 pts) | Medium (2 pts) | Low (1 pt) |
|-----------|-------------|----------------|-----------|
| **Role Level** | VP/Head/Director | Manager/Lead | Specialist/Coordinator |
| **Budget Authority** | Direct budget owner | Influences budget | No budget authority |
| **Company Stage** | Launch/Growth phase | Mature portfolio | Declining/restructuring |
| **Therapeutic Fit** | Immunology, Virology, Oncology | Adjacent areas | Unrelated |
| **Recent Activity** | New product launch, FDA approval | Pipeline advancement | Stable operations |
| **Geographic Fit** | France, Lyon area | Francophone Europe | Other |

**Priority Tiers:**
- **Tier 1** (15-18 pts): Immediate outreach
- **Tier 2** (11-14 pts): Priority follow-up
- **Tier 3** (7-10 pts): Nurture campaign
- **Tier 4** (<7 pts): Low priority

### Red Flags (Disqualify)
- Company in major restructuring/layoffs
- Role focused on commercial/sales (not medical)
- No products in development or approved
- Company has large in-house medical writing team

## Research Workflow

### Step 1: Company Identification
1. Start with industry news for recent launches/approvals
2. Check ClinicalTrials.gov for active sponsors
3. Review conference exhibitor lists

### Step 2: Contact Discovery
1. Search LinkedIn for company + role keywords
2. Check company website leadership pages
3. Review press releases for named individuals
4. Search publication databases for authors

### Step 3: Enrichment
1. Read LinkedIn profiles thoroughly
2. Check for recent posts/activity
3. Look for mutual connections
4. Review company news for context

### Step 4: Qualification
1. Score against qualification matrix
2. Identify specific pain points
3. Note recent triggers (launch, approval, hiring)
4. Prioritize by tier

### Step 5: Outreach Preparation
1. Customize message template
2. Reference specific company news/challenges
3. Prepare value proposition alignment
4. Set follow-up cadence

## Key Takeaways

1. **Focus on Medical Affairs, Scientific Affairs, Regulatory Affairs** - These are SciSense's core buyers

2. **Prioritize decision makers** (VP/Head level) but nurture influencers (MSLs, Project Managers)

3. **Use company news as conversation starters** - Product launches, FDA approvals, partnerships

4. **LinkedIn is the primary research tool** - Combine with company websites and conference data

5. **Qualify rigorously** - Focus effort on high-potential prospects

6. **Personalize based on research** - Reference specific challenges and news

7. **Track everything** - Build institutional knowledge for long-term relationship development
