2. Graphical Abstract/Video Abstract are Needed

Authors are encouraged to provide a graphical representation of the
paper as a self-explanatory image to appear alongside the abstract
appearing on the Table of Contents, or to provide a video abstract
instead. This will help attract more readers and increase the visibility
of your paper once it is published online.
1. The GA shouldn't include a title, affiliations, or refs;
2. The GA shouldn't be the same as a figure in the manuscript;
3. There shouldn't be a long block of text in the GA;
4. The GA shouldn't be a simple combination of the Abstract and a
Picture (even just a figure from the main text);
5. The GA shouldn't have any copyright issues;
6. The reader should be able to easily read the smallest font size of a
character, number, or symbol;
7. The GA should be a high-quality illustration or diagram in any one of
the following formats: PNG, JPEG, or TIFF.
8. The minimum required size for the GA should be 560 × 1100 pixels
(height × width). When submitting larger images, please make sure to
keep to the same ratio.
A video abstract is a short video that is used on the article online
page to introduce the article in general.
File formats: mp4, webm, ogv (mp4 format is usually recommended)
File size: no more than 200 MB
Video length: no more than 5 minutes

Examples:
https://www.mdpi.com/2227-9067/11/1/44
https://www.mdpi.com/2227-9067/11/1/115
If the video abstract is too large to upload, we suggest using an
attachment.
Please kindly *give us a short reply* to confirm with us if you are
willing to provide a graphic/video abstract to present your research.

Please feel free to contact us if you have any questions. We look
forward to your reply.
